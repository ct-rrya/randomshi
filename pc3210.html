Business Processes

Definitions & Types
Business Process: A collection of related, structured activities performed by people or systems to deliver a specific outcome for a defined audience.[1][2]
Three Types
  • Core Processes — directly create customer value (e.g., delivering a service, processing a sale)
  • Support Processes — enable core processes (e.g., HR, IT infrastructure, payroll)
  • Management Processes — govern the organization (e.g., strategic planning, compliance)[1]
Key Characteristics[1]
Defined Input / Output Repeatable Measurable Value-Adding
Presenter 2 of 7 · Dela Pieza, Frances Mae

IT & Business Processes

How IT Supports Them
"IT is a means to an end. The end is always the business process."[3]
5 Ways IT Supports Processes
  • Automation — eliminates repetitive manual tasks[1]
  • Communication — email, video conferencing, collaboration tools[3]
  • Data Management — fast, accurate information retrieval at scale[4]
  • Decision Support — analytics and reporting for informed choices[1]
  • Security & Compliance — protects process integrity and legal requirements[5]
Failure Modes
  • Automating a broken process just makes you fail faster
  • Technology-first thinking — choosing tools before understanding needs
Presenter 3 of 7 · Delos Santos, Bea

The IT Environment

5 Core Components
The complete ecosystem of hardware, software, networks, data, and people that an organization uses to manage information.[4]
Hardware

Servers, PCs, routers, switches, IoT devices — the physical foundation

Software

OS, ERP, CRM, custom apps — what the hardware actually does

Network

LAN, WAN, Wi-Fi, VPN — connects devices, enables data flow

Data

Records, documents, logs — most valuable and most targeted asset

People

IT staff, users, admins — most unpredictable component

Key Point[4]

A failure in any one component cascades through all the others.

Presenter 4 of 7 · Doble, Vence Peter

IT Infrastructure

Types & Security Layers
TypeAdvantageRisk
On-Premise Full control, no internet dependency High cost, needs in-house staff
Cloud-Based Scalable, lower maintenance Internet-dependent, off-site data
Hybrid Flexibility + balance of control Complex to manage
Defense in Depth[5]
No single layer is enough. Layer defenses so if one fails, the next holds.
  • Physical → Network → Application → Data → People[5]
  • RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act) applies to all IT professionals in PH[12]

Presenter 6 of 7 · Edaño, Merry Apple

Managing IT Change

Culture & Change Management
Most IT failures are human failures, not technical ones.[8]
Why People Resist[8]
  • "We've always done it this way" — routine feels safer
  • Fear of job displacement from automation
  • Low digital literacy — avoid admitting confusion
  • No leadership buy-in — staff follows leadership's lead
6 Strategies[8]
Involve users early Communicate the why Train thoroughly Pilot group first Secure leadership support Celebrate wins
Presenter 7 of 7 · Fernandez, Kirt Irah

Professionalism in IT

Traits, Ethics & Professional Behavior
Conduct consistent with professional norms and ethics — especially when no one is watching.[9]
6 Core Traits[9]
Reliability Accountability Integrity Competence Respect Confidentiality
Ethics Codes
  • ACM — contribute to society, avoid harm, honesty, fairness, privacy[10]
  • IEEE — public safety, honesty in claims, no conflicts of interest[11]
  • RA 10173 — Data Privacy Act of 2012, Philippine law for IT professionals[12]
Why It Matters Organizationally
  • Professionalism builds trust → more influence and access within the org
